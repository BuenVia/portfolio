Using Verbs

Indicate an action

A verb is a word that indicates someone is 'doing' or ''being'. It is an action word

A 'doing' word describs an action.

Matt runs three times per week.

A 'being' word describes the state, quality of carachteristic of something.

Matt is someone who enjoys running.

Infinitive = Basic

The inifinitive is the basic form of the verb. The infinitive will only tell you what action is occuring.

The conjugation is the change made to indicate who done what, and when.

Pronouns

The person (or the thing) which is doing the verb. In Spanish, it is NOT common to use the pronoun as the verb conjugation indicates who is doing the action.

Singular Plural
First Person I We
Second Person You You (all)
Third Person He / She / It They

Conjugation

The verb changes it's form to indicate who is doing the action, and when they are doing it.

Past Present Future
I went to the shops I go to the shops I will go to the shops
You went to the shops You go to the shops You will go to the shops
We went to the shops We go to the shops We will go to the shops

The main tense

As explained above, a conjugated verb will indicate who has done the action and when. The 'when' is also known as the 'Tense'. The tenses used in Spanish are:

Past When is it used Example
Preterite When something occured suddenly, or it has started and completed in the past. I ran five KMs on Wednesday
Imperfect An action that occured in the past, regularly, or happened over an undefined period of time I used to go running on Wednesdays
Perfect Something that occurred recently and has just finished. I have ran 5 kilometers today.
Pluperfect Something has occured in the past, but something else relevant has occured since then. I had run 5 kilometers.
Present When is it used Example
Present Something that is occuring now, or happens repeatedly. I run fifteen kilometers every week
Present Continuous Something that is occurring right now. I am running a race.
Future When is it used Example
Immediate Future Something is going to happen imminently. I am going to run fifteen kilometers this week
Proper Future Something will happen at some point in the future. I will run a race.
Conditional Something that might occur in the future. I would run if...

Moods

The mood indicates if it is factual, uncertain, or a command.

Mood When it is used Example
Indicative When something is certain to occur, or have occurred. I run 5 kilometers everyday.
Imperative A command or a request Run, now!
Subjunctive When there is doubt or you want to express your wish/ desire for it to happen. I hope I can go for a run today.

Infinitive

Dictionary verbs are infinitive

Verbs in the dictionary are always in the infinitive.

Spanish verbs in the infinitive always end in either -AR, -ER or -IR,

Hablar. Comer. Venir.

Infinitves are used when following other verbs

When using two verbs together, the second verb is usually infinitive.

I want to run today

Quiero correr hoy

Other inifintive uses

Sometimes an infinitive can be used at the begining of the sentence.

Ver el fútbol me hace feliz

Watching football makes me happy
